Job #1: Computer Science Tutor in the CSLC

The first Computer Science job I had was being a tutor in the CSLC (Computer Science Learning Center) where I would help out computer science students at UW-Madison with programs and teaching general programming concepts in primarily Java but additionally Python and C My dedication to the students and ability to explain complicated concepts while tutoring was recognized by the professor who runs the CSLC which is how I got the job as the Peer Mentor for CS400!

The CSLC has been recognized by the department in an article you can see here

Job #2: Lead Instructor at iD Tech/Summer Internship

Over the summer, I was an instructor for a small group of female students to introduce concepts in Artificial Intelligence through a Cozmo robot and programming in Python as well as an introductory VEX robotics course with programming in Modkit and C++. The young women worked on projects that both showed off their programming skills and their passion for making the world a better place. One example of a project was one student created a minigame where her robot drives up to objects and has people guess whether an object is recyclable or not. Another student had her robot create a home out of blocks and inform people about homelessness.

Job #3: Research Assistant and Wordpress Developer at NASA GeneLab

I participate in the NASA GeneLab, a project within NASA aimed at making molecular data about how life adapts to spaceflight more readily available.
